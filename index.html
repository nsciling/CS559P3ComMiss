Command Missile

Background

This game was made by Nick Scilingo as a coding project for UW-Madison's CS559-Computer Graphics course, spring 2024.
This game is a take on Missile Command, a classic arcade game from Atari.
This was made for purely educational purposes.

Game Premise

Your city is under attack!
Prevent enemy missiles from hitting your city. Use your anti-ballistic missiles (ABMs) to protect the city.
Stop enemy missles from reaching the city by blowing them up with your ABMs.

Controls

Press S to start the game.
Press ESC to pause.
Left click to fire an anti-ballistic missile. You have 9 missles that reload after they explode.

Scoring

For every enemy missile you defeat, you will gain 1 point. Every 10 points you gain, enemy intensity will increase.
If an enemy missile hits your town, you will lose city health. Different enemy types affect health differently.

If your city loses all of its health, GAME OVER!
